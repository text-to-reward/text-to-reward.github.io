Dense Reward Generation For Robot Learning

Anonymous authors1,
1The University of Hong Kong
Paper Code Twitter Video

Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) have been shown to be useful planners for robotics, but they may lack low-level skills for execution. On the other hand, reinforcement learning (RL) can learn low-level skills just from rewards, while the sensitivity and efficiency of RL training have made reward design notoriously laborious. In this work, we propose a novel framework, Text2Reward, to incorporate LLMs into robot learning. Given a language goal, Text2Reward generates free-form, dense reward codes for RL training interactively with human feedback. In this framework, users require no foundational knowledge of robotics or programming. Instead, they are only mandated to delineate the objective and offer feedback on the robot's execution, all in natural language. Different from hand-crafted APIs, our free-form dense reward is highly expressive and thus can generalize to novel tasks and utilize available tools (e.g. NumPy operations point clouds from a depth camera). We systematically evaluate our framework on two manipulation benchmarks (ManiSkill2, MetaWorld) and two locomotion environments of MuJoCo. The results show that the performance of RL policies trained with generated reward codes can match that of ground truth reward codes carefully tuned by human experts with heavy effort, as well as performing novel tasks. We demonstrate that the trained policy can be deployed on a real Franka Panda robot. With human feedback, our method can iteratively improve the learned policy and eliminate ambiguity.
